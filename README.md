## Prompt 2 — Long-Form Blog Content (Search-Intent Optimized)

**Prompt:**
> Write the introduction and first two sections (H2s) of the blog above in a helpful, easy-to-read tone for homeowners. Keep it practical and avoid jargon.

**Output:**

**Introduction**
A small plumbing issue rarely stays small. What starts as a minor drip or a faint gurgling sound can quickly turn into a costly repair if ignored. Knowing the early warning signs can save you time, money, and stress — and help you decide exactly when it's time to call in a professional.

**Why Ignoring Plumbing Issues Can Cost You More**
Many homeowners delay calling a plumber because the problem doesn't seem urgent. But plumbing issues rarely fix themselves. A tiny leak behind a wall can lead to mold growth and structural damage over time, while a partially clogged drain often signals a deeper blockage forming in your pipes.

**Sign #1 — Persistent Low Water Pressure**
If your shower or taps have noticeably weaker water flow than usual, it could point to a hidden leak, mineral buildup, or a problem with your main water line. While low pressure is sometimes harmless, persistent issues should be checked by a professional to rule out bigger concerns.
